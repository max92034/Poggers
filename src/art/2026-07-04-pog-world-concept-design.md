# POG World Concept Design

## Summary

This concept establishes a far-future society in the year 30xx where humanity, exhausted by 11 devastating proxy wars, has universally agreed to settle geopolitical conflict through ritualized `pog` competition rather than conventional war. The visual direction should not present this as a joke world or a collapsed dystopia. It should instead feel like a highly functional first-world civilization that has normalized an absurd and emotionally corrosive social contract.

The concept board should be environment-first and built as a layered world sheet rather than a single hero illustration. The surface world reads as calm, attractive, nostalgic, and domestic. Under that surface, the same city reveals neglected service corridors, economic stress, exploitative side markets, and a criminal underground shaped directly by the culture of sanctioned toy-based conflict.

## Goal

Create a canvas-ready concept direction for:

- world background
- residential architecture
- landscape and urban atmosphere
- fashion-adjacent visual cues
- darker social corners
- criminal underground culture

The first output should prioritize environmental storytelling over character close-ups while leaving space for later fashion and character expansion.

## Core Creative Thesis

The world should feel like `2000s anime future technology` trapped inside `1000-year-old real estate`.

Key contrast:

- buildings are ancient, over-renovated, inherited, patched, and continuously adapted
- daily consumer tech is elegant, advanced, compact, translucent, and slightly optimistic
- public life feels stable and aesthetically maintained
- the deeper one looks, the more the city reveals repression, scarcity, rigged systems, and criminal opportunism

This contrast is the foundation of the visual identity.

## Narrative World Logic

### Historical context

- Humanity has endured 11 major proxy wars.
- Each war lasted at least a decade.
- Major powers were historically insulated from the worst direct damage.
- At some point the world collectively accepted a rule that conflict will be decided through `pog` competition.
- Society is extremely serious about this rule.

### Demographic and social effects

- Male-to-female ratio is roughly `1:10`.
- There are relatively few elderly people still alive.
- Youth culture, reproductive pressure, social performance, and public ritual likely dominate visual culture.
- Civil order is not broken; it is over-organized and emotionally distorted.

### Environmental implication

The city should not look bombed out. It should look inherited, maintained, and socially engineered. Signs of historical trauma appear through memorialization, public ritual, and structural stress rather than obvious ruin.

## Recommended Visual Direction

### Primary mode

`Soft Retro-Future Urbanism`

This is the recommended base direction because it best supports the user’s desired mix of residence design, landscape, social atmosphere, and hidden corruption.

Characteristics:

- dense residential megablocks
- incremental renovation across centuries
- enclosed balconies and patchwork exterior updates
- external pipes, utility cages, rooftop additions, service bridges
- domestic urban density rather than monumental sci-fi spectacle
- appealing consumer-facing design language in public areas and personal objects

### Secondary hidden strata

The city should also clearly include:

- dark, seedy corners of society
- criminal underground systems

These must feel like extensions of the same civilization, not separate genres.

## Canvas Structure

The concept board should be organized into six visual zones.

### 1. Hero residential skyline

Purpose:
Establish the world’s main reading at first glance.

Content:

- thousand-year-old apartment towers with visible renovation eras
- layered facades with mixed materials from different centuries
- laundry cages, enclosed balconies, solar skins, patched tiles, old concrete, retrofit glass
- elevated pedestrian paths, rooftop utility structures, transit lines, stacked signage
- residential massing that feels lived in, expensive, inherited, and hard to replace

Emotional read:

- calm
- familiar
- slightly nostalgic
- quietly uncanny

### 2. Domestic future tech strip

Purpose:
Show that everyday objects progressed even while architecture remained burdened by the past.

Content:

- transparent communicators
- soft-glow interface devices
- modular identity cards and payment tools
- automated kitchen and grooming devices
- projection mirrors and personal AI companions
- elegant anime-like portable tech with rounded, optimistic industrial design

Design note:

Avoid military-looking hardware. The everyday technology should feel consumer-grade, desirable, and integrated into normal life.

### 3. Public ideology and social signage

Purpose:
Express the social order visually without exposition-heavy text.

Content:

- pog tournament advertisements
- etiquette posters about fair conflict ritual
- state-approved youth programs and civic branding
- war memorial graphics presented with clean institutional polish
- public iconography that treats competitive play with ceremonial gravity

Tone:

- cheerful on the surface
- disturbingly official on second look

### 4. Material and color lane

Purpose:
Define the texture vocabulary so the board feels unified.

Old-world materials:

- weathered concrete
- ceramic tile
- painted steel
- repaired stone
- stained plaster

Future-facing materials:

- translucent polymers
- glossy sealed surfaces
- soft illuminated acrylic
- holographic labeling
- color-changing smart panels

Recommended surface palette:

- dusty cream
- faded concrete gray
- oxidized teal
- vending-machine cyan
- sunset pink
- signal red accents

### 5. Seedy undercity slice

Purpose:
Reveal the unofficial underside of the same residential system.

Content:

- damp alley service corridors below megablocks
- illegal subdivided rooms
- repair kiosks assembled from scavenged tech
- unauthorized clinics, temporary shelters, hidden food stalls
- failing lights, leaking pipes, half-maintained stairwells, wet ground surfaces
- people living in the cracks of an otherwise polished urban order

Design note:

This area should still visually belong to the main city. Use the same structural bones, but in poorer light, worse maintenance, and denser clutter.

Recommended undercity palette:

- sodium orange
- dirty magenta
- mold green
- flickering cyan
- oil-slick black
- wet concrete gray

### 6. Criminal underground vignette

Purpose:
Show the organized shadow economy generated by the world’s formal rules.

Content:

- illegal pog gambling dens
- counterfeit official caps
- match-fixing operations
- brokers influencing sanctioned conflict outcomes
- black-market trade in restricted tech, identity records, reproductive commodities, and war memorabilia
- meeting rooms inside abandoned mall strata, sealed transit platforms, maintenance tunnels, forgotten commercial basements

Creative guardrail:

Do not default to generic cyberpunk gangs. Crime here should emerge from ritualized conflict, demographic imbalance, state control, and scarcity inside a high-functioning society.

## Tone and Mood

The concept should hold three layers at once:

- `surface`: attractive, calm, domestic, normalized
- `underside`: humid, cramped, compromised, economically stressed
- `core corruption`: stylish, ritualized, transactional, quietly dangerous

The world should feel:

- beautiful at first glance
- unsettling on closer inspection
- socially coherent rather than random

## Architectural Language

The architecture should suggest that the `2008 housing bubble never truly collapsed` and that real estate became a permanent inherited burden. Over centuries, buildings were never meaningfully replaced, only renovated, expanded, subdivided, sealed, repainted, re-skinned, and technologically retrofitted.

Architectural cues:

- persistent structural shells from old eras
- visible strata of renovation history
- valuable but aging housing stock
- dense habitation inside old bones
- premium façades in public-facing areas, neglect in service-facing areas
- improvised adaptation where regulation and reality diverge

## Fashion and Human Presence

Even though this first concept is environment-first, the board should include light fashion cues through silhouettes and crowd logic.

Guidelines:

- female-majority crowd presence should be noticeable
- youth and young adults dominate public imagery
- elderly figures are rare
- clothing should combine practical citywear with polished consumer-tech accents
- fashion should feel slightly stylized and anime-adjacent, but still urban and wearable

Avoid turning this into a runway sheet. Human figures should support the world rather than become the main focus.

## What To Avoid

- generic neon cyberpunk clichés
- post-apocalyptic ruin aesthetics
- parody or overt comedy treatment
- ultra-clean utopian sci-fi towers with no history
- militarized sci-fi hardware dominating the frame
- visually separating the underworld so much that it feels like a different city

## Success Criteria

The concept direction is successful if:

- the viewer immediately understands the world as retro-future and residential
- the city feels old but continuously inhabited
- the consumer tech feels more advanced than the architecture
- the public order feels both stable and ideologically strange
- the seedy corners and underground feel organically connected to the main city
- the overall tone is compelling rather than ironic

## Execution Recommendation

The first canvas deliverable should be a `layered world board` rather than a single cinematic splash image.

Recommended order of emphasis:

1. hero residential world background
2. embedded public ritual and pog society cues
3. domestic technology language
4. seedy service-level underside
5. criminal underground vignettes
6. supporting human silhouettes and fashion hints

This order ensures that the world remains legible as a society first and a genre collage second.
